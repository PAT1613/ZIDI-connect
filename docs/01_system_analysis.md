# 01 — System Analysis

> ZIDI Connect — Customer Management, Service, Invoicing, Notification & Reporting Platform.
> This document is the analytical foundation that the design (`02_system_design.md`),
> database (`03_database_design.md`), and implementation chapters refer back to.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

ZIDI Connect is a back-office operating system for small-to-medium service providers (Internet
Service Providers, utility resellers, SaaS distributors, equipment-leasing firms) whose
core business is recurring service delivery against a known customer base. The platform
unifies five operational concerns that are commonly fragmented across spreadsheets, WhatsApp
groups, bank SMS alerts, and ad-hoc email threads:

1. **Customer mastery** — a single, deduplicated customer record keyed by `national_id`.
2. **Service catalogue & subscription tracking** — every subscription carries an explicit
   `due_date`, `renewal_date`, and `auto_renew` flag.
3. **Invoicing & payments** — invoices are auto-issued on subscription creation and renewal,
   payments are reconciled against them, and status flips through `pending → paid → overdue`
   are driven by the system rather than by humans remembering.
4. **Outbound communications** — single and bulk SMS (via Africa's Talking) and email (SMTP)
   to customers, with full delivery audit.
5. **Automated reminders & escalations** — a Celery Beat scheduler scans subscriptions hourly
   and dispatches reminders at 14, 7, and 3 days before `due_date`, then escalates anything
   that crosses `due_date` unpaid to a round-robin queue of Customer Service Officers.

The platform is delivered as a modular Django 5 / DRF 3.15 backend, a React 19 + Vite SPA,
and a PostgreSQL 16 + Redis 7 data plane, packaged behind Nginx + Gunicorn on a single
Ubuntu 22.04 VPS. The locked technical contract is `TECH_SPEC.md`; this document never
diverges from it.

For the academic reader this is also a final-year project deliverable. It demonstrates
applied software engineering practice across requirements analysis, system modelling,
relational design, REST API design, event-driven background processing, role-based
security, automated testing, and production deployment with TLS, backups, and rollback.

## 2. Problem Statement

Service-based SMEs in the East African market share a recurring operational pattern:

- Customer details live in a mixture of paper forms, WhatsApp contacts, and Excel sheets
  that drift out of sync within weeks of onboarding.
- Subscription renewal dates are tracked manually. When the staff member who "owned" a
  customer leaves or goes on leave, that customer's renewal is silently missed.
- Invoices are generated by hand in Word, exported to PDF, and emailed individually. There
  is no canonical record of which invoice was sent to whom and when.
- Payment confirmation depends on bank SMS forwarding to a manager's phone, who then tells
  the finance officer who marks the customer "paid" in a spreadsheet — a three-step chain
  with no audit trail.
- Reminders for upcoming renewals are sent ad-hoc, usually only after the customer has
  already lapsed, leading to involuntary churn that customers themselves did not want.
- Escalations have no system of record. The same overdue account may be chased by three
  different staff or by nobody at all.
- Management reporting (revenue, churn, communications volume) requires a full day of
  spreadsheet reconciliation each month.

The combined effect is **avoidable revenue loss, regulatory risk (no audit trail for
financial events), and a poor customer experience** driven not by neglect but by lack of
tooling.

ZIDI Connect resolves these by:

1. Making the customer record the **single, immutable source of truth**, with all events
   (subscription, invoice, payment, communication, escalation) hanging off it.
2. Replacing human memory with **scheduled background jobs** that fire reminders and
   escalations on a fixed cadence.
3. Replacing spreadsheets with a **role-gated web UI** so finance sees finance data,
   operations sees operations data, and management sees consolidated dashboards.
4. Generating **PDF / Excel reports on demand** in place of manual reconciliation.
5. Maintaining a tamper-resistant **AuditLog** of every write operation for forensic and
   regulatory traceability.

## 4. Functional Requirements

The functional scope mirrors the ten modules already declared in `TECH_SPEC.md` §2 and §3.
Each FR is uniquely numbered for traceability into the test suite (`07_testing.md`).

### FR-001 — Authentication & Session
- FR-001.1 The system shall allow registered users to obtain a JWT access + refresh token
  pair via `POST /api/v1/auth/login/` using email and password.
- FR-001.2 The system shall allow refresh of the access token via `POST /api/v1/auth/refresh/`.
- FR-001.3 The system shall expose `POST /api/v1/auth/logout/` to blacklist the refresh
  token.
- FR-001.4 The system shall expose `GET /api/v1/auth/me/` returning the current user, role,
  and permissions.

### FR-002 — User & Role Management
- FR-002.1 Only Super Admin shall create, update, deactivate users.
- FR-002.2 The system shall expose the five fixed roles defined in `TECH_SPEC.md` §4:
  Super Admin, Customer Service Officer, Finance Officer, Operations Officer, Manager.
- FR-002.3 The system shall enforce the role-permission matrix on every API call.

### FR-003 — Customer Management
- FR-003.1 The system shall auto-generate `customer_code` in the form `CUS-000001`.
- FR-003.2 The system shall enforce `national_id` uniqueness across the customer base.
- FR-003.3 The system shall support search by `full_name`, `phone`, `email`, `national_id`,
  and `customer_code`.
- FR-003.4 The system shall support soft-deactivation (`status=inactive`) but never
  hard-delete customers with linked invoices or payments.
- FR-003.5 The system shall paginate customer lists at 25 per page by default.

### FR-004 — Service Catalogue
- FR-004.1 The system shall maintain a catalogue of `Service` records with `name`, `price`,
  `billing_cycle` (monthly/quarterly/annual/one-off), and `sla_days`.
- FR-004.2 `service_code` shall be auto-generated as `SVC-000001`.
- FR-004.3 Operations Officer and Super Admin shall create/update services; other roles
  shall read only.

### FR-005 — Subscriptions (CustomerService)
- FR-005.1 The system shall allow a customer to be subscribed to one or more services, each
  represented as a `CustomerService` record with `start_date`, `due_date`, `renewal_date`,
  `status`, and `auto_renew`.
- FR-005.2 On subscription creation the system shall auto-issue an `Invoice` (FR-006.2).
- FR-005.3 The system shall expose actions: `renew`, `suspend`, `terminate`.
- FR-005.4 Subscription status shall flip to `expired` when `due_date < today` and unpaid.

### FR-006 — Invoicing
- FR-006.1 The system shall auto-generate `invoice_number` as `INV-000001`.
- FR-006.2 The system shall create an `Invoice` upon subscription creation, renewal, and
  upon manual addition by Finance.
- FR-006.3 Each invoice carries `amount`, `tax`, `total`, `issued_date`, `due_date`,
  `status` (pending/paid/overdue/cancelled), and an optional `notes` field.
- FR-006.4 The system shall expose `GET /api/v1/invoices/<id>/pdf/` returning a PDF render
  of the invoice (via `reportlab`).
- FR-006.5 Status shall flip to `overdue` when `due_date < today` and `paid_amount < total`.

### FR-007 — Payments
- FR-007.1 The system shall record payments against invoices with `amount`, `method`
  (mobile_money/bank_transfer/cash), `reference`, `paid_at`, and `received_by` user.
- FR-007.2 When the sum of payments equals or exceeds invoice total, invoice flips to
  `paid` and any open escalation for the underlying subscription closes.
- FR-007.3 Finance Officer and Super Admin shall create payments; other roles shall read
  only.

### FR-008 — Communications (manual outbound)
- FR-008.1 The system shall send single and bulk SMS via Africa's Talking through
  `POST /api/v1/communications/sms/`.
- FR-008.2 The system shall send single and bulk email through
  `POST /api/v1/communications/email/`.
- FR-008.3 Every send shall be persisted as a `CommunicationLog` row with `sender`,
  `customer`, `channel`, `message`, `status`, `sent_at`.
- FR-008.4 The system shall expose a delivery-status callback at
  `POST /api/v1/communications/sms/callback/` for Africa's Talking webhooks.

### FR-009 — Notification Engine (automated)
- FR-009.1 Celery Beat shall invoke `apps.notifications.tasks.scan_due_subscriptions`
  hourly.
- FR-009.2 For each active `CustomerService` with `due_date - today ∈ {14,7,3}` the system
  shall enqueue a reminder over SMS + Email + in-app, deduped on
  `(subscription_id, interval)`.
- FR-009.3 For each active `CustomerService` with `due_date < today` and unpaid, the
  system shall set `status=expired`, enqueue an overdue notice, and create an
  `Escalation` (status `open`) assigned by round-robin to active CS Officers.
- FR-009.4 Each notification shall be persisted to `Notification` with `status`
  (queued/sent/failed/delivered) and `provider_message_id`.
- FR-009.5 Failed sends shall retry 3× with exponential backoff (60 s, 5 min, 30 min).

### FR-010 — Escalations
- FR-010.1 The system shall maintain `Escalation` records with `customer_service`,
  `assigned_to`, `status` (open/in_progress/resolved/closed), `opened_at`, `resolved_at`,
  and `notes`.
- FR-010.2 Assignment shall be round-robin across active Customer Service Officers.
- FR-010.3 Resolution shall be automatic when the underlying invoice transitions to `paid`,
  otherwise manual via PATCH.

### FR-011 — Reporting
- FR-011.1 The system shall expose `GET /api/v1/reports/dashboard/` returning JSON KPIs:
  active customers, revenue MTD, invoices outstanding, notifications sent (last 7 days),
  open escalations.
- FR-011.2 The system shall expose reports for customers, services, revenue, invoices,
  payments, and notifications, each accepting `?format=pdf|xlsx`.
- FR-011.3 PDF exports use `reportlab`, XLSX uses `openpyxl`.

### FR-012 — Audit Log
- FR-012.1 Every create/update/delete on the ten core entities shall write an `AuditLog`
  row with `user`, `action`, `module`, `object_repr`, `old_value`, `new_value`,
  `ip_address`, `created_at`.
- FR-012.2 `GET /api/v1/audit-logs/` shall be read-only and restricted to Super Admin and
  Manager.

## 7. User Stories

Each story is INVEST-compliant (Independent, Negotiable, Valuable, Estimable, Small,
Testable). Acceptance criteria are summarised; full Gherkin scenarios live in
`07_testing.md` §7.

### Super Admin
- **US-001** As a Super Admin I want to create a user and assign a role so that a new staff
  member can log in with the right permissions. *Accept:* user receives credentials,
  `users/` POST returns 201, login succeeds.
- **US-002** As a Super Admin I want to deactivate a user so that a departed employee
  cannot access the system. *Accept:* `is_active=False`, subsequent login returns 401.
- **US-003** As a Super Admin I want to view the audit log so that I can investigate who
  changed a customer record. *Accept:* `GET /audit-logs/?customer_id=...` returns chronological entries.
- **US-004** As a Super Admin I want to reset another user's password (via Django admin) so
  that I can recover access when an officer is locked out.
- **US-005** As a Super Admin I want to see live dashboard KPIs so that I have a single
  health view of the platform.

### Customer Service Officer
- **US-006** As a CS Officer I want to register a new customer with `full_name`, `phone`,
  `email`, `national_id`, and `address` so that they enter the master record.
  *Accept:* unique `national_id`, `customer_code` auto-generated, 201 returned.
- **US-007** As a CS Officer I want to search customers by phone so that I can identify
  the caller within seconds.
- **US-008** As a CS Officer I want to subscribe a customer to a service so that invoicing
  and reminders begin automatically.
- **US-009** As a CS Officer I want to send a bulk SMS to a filtered list of customers so
  that I can announce a service window in one action.
- **US-010** As a CS Officer I want to see escalations assigned to me, sorted by
  `opened_at`, so that I work the oldest first.
- **US-011** As a CS Officer I want to mark an escalation `in_progress` and record notes
  so that my colleagues see the latest action.
- **US-012** As a CS Officer I want to deactivate a customer when they formally cancel.

### Finance Officer
- **US-013** As a Finance Officer I want to view all `pending` and `overdue` invoices,
  oldest first, so that I can prioritise collections.
- **US-014** As a Finance Officer I want to record a payment against an invoice so that
  the system flips its status to `paid` and closes any escalation.
- **US-015** As a Finance Officer I want to download an invoice as PDF so that I can
  forward it on demand.
- **US-016** As a Finance Officer I want to issue a manual ad-hoc invoice (not tied to a
  subscription) so that I can charge a one-off setup fee.
- **US-017** As a Finance Officer I want a monthly revenue report as XLSX so that I can
  reconcile against the bank statement.
- **US-018** As a Finance Officer I want a payments report by `method` so that I can split
  mobile-money vs bank-transfer volumes.

### Operations Officer
- **US-019** As an Operations Officer I want to add a new service to the catalogue so that
  CS can sell it.
- **US-020** As an Operations Officer I want to change the price of a service so that the
  next subscription captures the new tariff (existing subscriptions retain their original
  price on issued invoices).
- **US-021** As an Operations Officer I want to suspend a customer's subscription with a
  reason so that service delivery halts without losing the record.
- **US-022** As an Operations Officer I want to renew a subscription with one click so that
  a new `due_date` is computed from the `billing_cycle`.

### Manager
- **US-023** As a Manager I want to view the dashboard so that I can monitor revenue,
  active customers, and open escalations in one screen.
- **US-024** As a Manager I want a customer report filtered by registration date so that
  I can measure growth.
- **US-025** As a Manager I want the notifications report so that I can see the volume
  and delivery success of automated reminders.
- **US-026** As a Manager I want read access to audit logs so that I can independently
  verify finance actions.

### Customer (external, receives only)
- **US-027** As a Customer I want to receive an SMS 14, 7, and 3 days before my
  subscription `due_date` so that I am never surprised by a service cut-off.
- **US-028** As a Customer I want to receive a payment receipt SMS immediately after my
  payment is posted so that I have written confirmation.

### System
- **US-029** As the System I want to scan subscriptions hourly so that no reminder is
  missed regardless of staff presence.
- **US-030** As the System I want to retry failed SMS three times so that transient
  provider errors do not silently drop notifications.

## 8. Use Cases (detailed)

Each use case follows the same template: actor, preconditions, main flow, alternates,
postconditions.

### UC-01 — Register Customer
- **Primary actor:** CS Officer
- **Preconditions:** Officer authenticated; not at customer create limit.
- **Main flow:**
  1. Officer opens *Customers → New*.
  2. Officer fills name, phone, email, `national_id`, address.
  3. UI POSTs to `/customers/`.
  4. API validates uniqueness of `national_id`.
  5. API assigns next `customer_code` (`CUS-000001`-style).
  6. API writes `Customer` row and an `AuditLog` (`action=create`).
  7. UI shows success toast and redirects to customer detail.
- **Alternate A:** `national_id` already exists → 400 with `{ national_id: ["already exists"] }`.
- **Postcondition:** Customer is searchable and can be subscribed.

### UC-02 — Subscribe Customer to Service
- **Primary actor:** CS Officer
- **Preconditions:** Customer is `active`. Service is `active`.
- **Main flow:**
  1. Officer opens customer detail and clicks *Add subscription*.
  2. Officer chooses service, `start_date`, `auto_renew`.
  3. API computes `due_date` = `start_date + billing_cycle` and `renewal_date` = `due_date`.
  4. API creates `CustomerService` (`status=active`).
  5. API auto-creates `Invoice` with `amount = service.price`, `tax`, `total`,
     `due_date = subscription.due_date`, `status=pending`.
  6. API writes two `AuditLog` rows.
- **Alternate A:** Customer inactive → 400.
- **Postcondition:** Subscription appears under customer; new pending invoice exists.

### UC-03 — Generate Invoice (manual)
- **Primary actor:** Finance Officer
- **Main flow:** Officer creates an `Invoice` with `customer_service=null`, sets `amount`,
  `tax`, `total`, `due_date`, optional `notes`. System persists and writes `AuditLog`.
- **Postcondition:** Invoice visible in finance queue, reachable by `customer_id` filter.

### UC-04 — Download Invoice PDF
- **Primary actor:** Finance Officer or Manager
- **Main flow:** GET `/invoices/<id>/pdf/` → server renders PDF via `reportlab` and streams
  it with `Content-Type: application/pdf` and a `Content-Disposition` filename of
  `INV-000123.pdf`.

### UC-05 — Record Payment
- **Primary actor:** Finance Officer
- **Preconditions:** Target invoice is `pending` or `overdue`.
- **Main flow:**
  1. Officer opens invoice detail, clicks *Record payment*.
  2. Officer enters `amount`, `method`, `reference`, `paid_at`.
  3. API writes `Payment` row with `received_by = current user`.
  4. API recalculates `sum(payments.amount)`. If `>= invoice.total`, invoice flips to
     `paid`.
  5. If invoice flips to `paid` **and** an `open`/`in_progress` `Escalation` exists for the
     same `customer_service`, that escalation flips to `resolved` with `resolved_at = now`.
  6. API enqueues a Celery task to send a payment-receipt SMS.
- **Postcondition:** Payment row, invoice status updated, possible escalation closed,
  receipt SMS queued.

### UC-06 — Send Bulk SMS
- **Primary actor:** CS Officer
- **Main flow:**
  1. Officer opens *Communications → Compose SMS*.
  2. Officer selects recipients (search filter, multi-select, or "all active customers").
  3. Officer enters message body (≤ 480 chars).
  4. POST `/communications/sms/` with `recipients: [customer_ids]`, `message`.
  5. API enqueues one Celery task per recipient (or batched), each writes a
     `CommunicationLog` row with `status=queued`.
  6. Africa's Talking responds with provider IDs; statuses are updated to `sent`.
  7. Delivery callback later updates to `delivered`/`failed`.

### UC-07 — Automated Reminder Send
- **Primary actor:** System
- **Trigger:** Celery Beat hourly.
- **Main flow:**
  1. `scan_due_subscriptions` queries `CustomerService.objects.filter(status='active', due_date__in=[today+14, today+7, today+3])`.
  2. For each subscription, for the matching interval, check absence of a `Notification`
     with `(customer_service_id, interval)` dedupe key.
  3. Enqueue `send_reminder(subscription_id, interval)` per channel (SMS, Email, in-app).
  4. Each channel task calls the relevant service (`SMSService`, `EmailService`,
     `InAppService`) and writes/updates a `Notification` row.
- **Alternates:** Provider error → retry (60 s, 5 min, 30 min); after third failure mark
  `Notification.status = failed`.

### UC-08 — Escalate Overdue Subscription
- **Primary actor:** System
- **Trigger:** Same hourly scan.
- **Main flow:**
  1. For each `CustomerService` with `due_date < today` and no `paid` invoice covering
     the period:
     a. Set `subscription.status = expired`.
     b. Enqueue an overdue notice (SMS + Email).
     c. Look up active CS Officers; pick the next in round-robin order (least open
        escalations, then oldest last assignment).
     d. Create an `Escalation` (`status=open`, `assigned_to=<picked user>`).
  2. Write `AuditLog` for the status change and the escalation.

### UC-09 — Resolve Escalation Manually
- **Primary actor:** CS Officer
- **Main flow:** PATCH `/escalations/<id>/` with `status=in_progress`, then later
  `status=resolved` plus `notes`. UI shows the escalation timeline.

### UC-10 — Export Report
- **Primary actor:** Manager (or role-specific officer)
- **Main flow:** GET `/reports/revenue/?format=pdf` (or `xlsx`). Server queries the
  underlying tables, formats with `reportlab` or `openpyxl`, streams the file.

### UC-11 — Login / Logout
- **Primary actor:** Any internal user
- **Main flow:** POST `/auth/login/` with `email`, `password` → `{access, refresh, user}`.
  Frontend stores tokens in memory + refresh in HttpOnly storage. On logout POST
  `/auth/logout/` with the refresh token to blacklist it.

### UC-12 — View Dashboard
- **Primary actor:** Manager / Super Admin
- **Main flow:** GET `/reports/dashboard/` → KPI JSON. Frontend renders cards + a recent
  activity feed (escalations opened in last 24 h, notifications sent last 7 d).

## 9. Business Rules

| ID | Rule |
|---|---|
| BR-001 | An `Invoice` is auto-issued on every subscription create and every subscription renew. |
| BR-002 | An `Invoice` flips to `paid` only when `sum(payments.amount) >= invoice.total`. Partial payments leave it `pending`. |
| BR-003 | An `Invoice` flips to `overdue` automatically when `due_date < today` and not paid. |
| BR-004 | Reminder cadence is fixed at 14, 7, and 3 days before `CustomerService.due_date`. |
| BR-005 | A reminder for `(customer_service, interval)` is sent **exactly once**. Duplicate triggers must no-op. |
| BR-006 | When `due_date < today` and unpaid, the subscription flips to `expired` and an `Escalation` is opened. |
| BR-007 | New escalations are assigned round-robin to active Customer Service Officers. |
| BR-008 | When an invoice flips to `paid`, any `open`/`in_progress` escalation on the same `customer_service` flips to `resolved`. |
| BR-009 | `customer_code`, `service_code`, and `invoice_number` are auto-generated and immutable. |
| BR-010 | `national_id` on `Customer` is globally unique. |
| BR-011 | `email` on `User` is globally unique and is the login username. |
| BR-012 | No financial record (`Invoice`, `Payment`) is ever hard-deleted; only `cancelled`. |
| BR-013 | Every create/update/delete on the ten core entities writes an `AuditLog` row. |
| BR-014 | The role-permission matrix in `TECH_SPEC.md` §4 is enforced on every API endpoint via `HasRolePermission`. |
| BR-015 | The Celery beat schedule runs `scan_due_subscriptions` hourly at minute 5. |
| BR-016 | Notification retries: 3 attempts at 60 s, 5 min, 30 min; then `status=failed`. |
| BR-017 | All money values stored as `DECIMAL(12,2)` to avoid float drift. |
| BR-018 | All timestamps stored UTC; displayed `Africa/Nairobi`. |
| BR-019 | A `Customer` with linked `Invoice`s cannot be deleted (FK `PROTECT`); it can only be deactivated. |
| BR-020 | Manual SMS / Email cannot be sent to customers whose `status=inactive` unless the sender confirms via UI override. |

## 10. Process Flows (textual)

### 10.1 Customer onboarding through first invoice
CS Officer registers customer (BR-009, BR-010) → CS Officer subscribes customer to service
→ `CustomerService` created with computed `due_date` → `Invoice` auto-created (BR-001) →
`AuditLog` rows for both → customer visible to all read-eligible roles.

### 10.2 Reminder lifecycle
T-14 days: hourly scan finds subscription → enqueues SMS+Email+InApp reminder; status
`queued → sent → delivered`. T-7 days and T-3 days repeat with dedupe by
`(subscription, interval)`. At T-0 unpaid: subscription → `expired`, overdue notice
queued, `Escalation` opened, round-robin assigned.

### 10.3 Payment & resolution
Finance posts payment → invoice flips `paid` when total met → linked open escalation
flips `resolved` (BR-008) → payment-receipt SMS queued → `AuditLog` rows for payment,
invoice transition, escalation transition.

### 10.4 Manual bulk communication
CS Officer composes SMS → recipients resolved server-side → one task per recipient
(or batched) → `CommunicationLog` rows + `Notification` rows where appropriate →
delivery callback updates rows.

### 10.5 Reporting cycle
Manager opens dashboard → KPI query runs against materialised counts → Manager exports
revenue report → `reporting` app queries `Payment` and `Invoice` joined to `Customer`,
formats via `openpyxl` or `reportlab`, streams response.
